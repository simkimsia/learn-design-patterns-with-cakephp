Learn Design Patterns with CakePHP

Using CakePHP as example to learn design patterns

Why Learn Design Patterns with CakePHP?

I am inspired by the advice given by CodingHorror aka Jeff Atwood on how to stop sucking and be awesome instead.

  1. Embrace the Suck
  2. Do it in Public
  3. Pick Stuff that Matters

So this is my way of embracing my suck publicly. My understanding of design patterns is poor. My mastery of CakePHP is only average. Hence to suck less at both, I have decided to create this as a learning tool to see how design patterns are specifically implemented in CakePHP --- the framework I am most familiar with.

Herein lies the danger. This is more for me to learn than for you to learn. You have been warned!

Design Patterns

I will go in the order of the Design Patterns introduced by the book HEAD FIRST on Design Patterns

  • Strategy Pattern
  • Observer Pattern
  • Decorator Pattern
  • Factory Pattern
  • Singleton Pattern
  • Command Pattern
  • Adapter and Facade Patterns
  • Template Method Pattern
  • Iterator and Composite Patterns
  • State Pattern
  • Proxy Pattern
  • Compound Patterns: Patterns of Patterns
